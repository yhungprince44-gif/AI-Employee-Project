## 🎯 Mission Refinement

Employee #1's mission was sharpened from *"find businesses"* to:

> **Find businesses that have a high chance of benefiting from a modern website.**

This shifts Employee #1 from a data collector into an opportunity evaluator.

## ✅ Decisions Made

- A business with **no website** is automatically qualified (reason: *"No Website"*).
- A business **with a website** goes through a structured Website Review Checklist before qualifying.
- Employee #1 evaluates opportunities using a **scored system**, not a binary yes/no.
- Employee #1 must judge whether it can build something *so much better* that the value is immediately obvious to the owner — if not, skip the business.

## 📜 Rules Approved

### Rule: Consultant Tone
Employee #1 must never say **"the website is bad."** Instead: **"This business has an opportunity to improve its online presence."** We are consultants, not critics.

### Website Review Checklist (6 Categories)
1. **Modern Design** — does it look outdated, attractive, trustworthy?
2. **Mobile Experience** — phone-friendly, readable, easy navigation?
3. **Speed** — fast loading, optimized images?
4. **User Experience** — can customers instantly tell what the business does, where it is, and how to contact/buy?
5. **Business Features** — booking, online ordering, WhatsApp button, live chat, price list, catalog, gallery, testimonials, FAQ, contact form, Google Maps, opening hours.
6. **Trust** — reviews, real photos, branding, professional appearance.

### Opportunity Scoring
Each category is scored (e.g. out of 10), rolling up into an **Overall Opportunity Score** (e.g. 8.7/10) instead of a simple pass/fail.

## 💡 Ideas Discussed

**Core principle established:**

> A business is worth contacting if we can create a significantly better online presence than what it currently has — or if it has no website at all.
